Activity Level

Activity level affects the calorie needed of a patient. Activity level depends on how physically active a patient's lifestyle is. Below is the lists of different activity level regarding to the patient’s daily activity such as their occupations.

Activity Level Description Example of Activities
Sedentary Little or no exercise / desk job
Less than 30 minutes of Light Activity (Cycling, Brisk Walking) per day		 Secretary, Clerk, Typist, Administrator, Cashier, Bank teller, Office Workers
Lightly Active Light exercise / sports 1-3 days/week
90+ minutes of Light Activity (Cycling, Brisk Walking) per day		 Teacher, Nurse, Salesman, Student, Laboratory Tech, House Wife with Maid
Moderately Active Moderate exercise / sports 3-5 days/week
9+ hours of Light Activity (Cycling, Brisk Walking) per day		 Housewife w/o Maid, Janitor, Waitress, Vendor, Mechanic, Jeepney and Car Driver
Very Active Heavy exercise / sports 6-7 days/week
4-5+ hours of Light Activity (Cycling, Brisk Walking) per day		 Construction, Landscaping, Farmer, Laborer, Cargador, Laborer, Coal Miner, Fishermen, Heavy Equipment Operator
